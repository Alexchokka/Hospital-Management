Health Emergencies and First Aid

Heart Attack

  • Symptoms: Chest pain, shortness of breath, dizziness, nausea, sweating.

First Aid Steps for Heart Attack:

  • Call emergency services immediately.
  • Keep the person calm and encourage them to rest.
  • If the person is conscious, give them aspirin to chew.
  • Perform CPR if the person becomes unresponsive.

Stroke

  • Symptoms: Sudden numbness or weakness in the face, arm, or leg, especially on one side of the body; confusion, trouble speaking or understanding speech.

First Aid Steps for Stroke:

  • Call emergency services immediately.
  • Keep the person calm and still.
  • Do not give them food, drink, or medication.
  • If the person is unconscious, place them in the recovery position.

Asthma Attack

  • Symptoms: Shortness of breath, wheezing, tightness in the chest, coughing.

First Aid Steps for Asthma Attack:

  • Help the person sit upright and remain calm.
  • Administer their prescribed inhaler or bronchodilator.
  • Encourage slow and deep breaths.
  • If the symptoms do not improve, seek medical attention immediately.

Seizures

  • Symptoms: Loss of consciousness, uncontrollable jerking movements, stiffening of the body.

First Aid Steps for Seizures:

  • Stay calm and ensure the person is in a safe place.
  • Do not try to restrain the person or put anything in their mouth.
  • Place a soft cushion under their head if possible.
  • Once the seizure stops, check for breathing and keep the person on their side.

Choking

  • Symptoms: Inability to breathe or speak, gasping for air, panic.

First Aid Steps for Choking:

  • Encourage the person to cough.
  • If the person is unable to cough, give them 5 back blows.
  • If they are still choking, perform the Heimlich maneuver.
  • If the person becomes unconscious, begin CPR and call emergency services immediately.

Burns

  • Symptoms: Red, swollen skin, blisters, intense pain.

First Aid Steps for Burns:

  • Cool the burn under cold running water for at least 10 minutes.
  • Cover the burn with a clean, non-stick bandage.
  • Avoid popping blisters.
  • Seek medical attention if the burn is severe.

Hypoglycemia (Low Blood Sugar)

  • Symptoms: Shaking, sweating, dizziness, confusion, weakness.

First Aid Steps for Hypoglycemia:

  • Give the person 15-20 grams of fast-acting sugar (e.g., juice, glucose tablets).
  • Wait 15 minutes and check their blood sugar.
  • If symptoms persist, give them another dose of sugar.
  • If they remain unresponsive, call emergency services.

Fractures

  • Symptoms: Pain, swelling, bruising, inability to move the affected limb.

First Aid Steps for Fractures:

  • Immobilize the broken limb using a splint or makeshift object.
  • Apply ice to reduce swelling.
  • Seek medical attention immediately.

Heat Stroke

  • Symptoms: High body temperature (above 40Â°C), confusion, rapid pulse, fainting.

First Aid Steps for Heat Stroke:

  • Move the person to a cool place and remove excess clothing.
  • Cool the body with cold packs, wet cloths, or a cool bath.
  • Encourage the person to drink cool fluids if they are conscious.
  • Call emergency services immediately.

Allergic Reaction

  • Symptoms: Swelling, hives, difficulty breathing, anaphylaxis.

First Aid Steps for Allergic Reaction:

  • Administer an epinephrine injection (if prescribed) immediately.
  • Call emergency services immediately.
  • If the person is having trouble breathing, keep them calm and have them sit upright.
